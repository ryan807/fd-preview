The problem

The hardest step a patient takes is the first one.

When someone finally reaches out for behavioral health care, the window is short. Calls go to voicemail after hours, forms get abandoned, and intake paperwork eats hours of staff time. Every delay is a person who may not call back, and a clinician buried in admin instead of care.

⏱

Inquiries go cold

After-hours and weekend inquiries wait for a callback that often comes too late. The moment of readiness passes.

📋

Intake is a bottleneck

Manual intake takes ~2 hours per patient, delays first appointments, and pulls staff away from clinical work.

⚠

Risk hides in the gaps

Critical clinical flags surface late, sometimes not until the patient is in the room, when they could have been caught at intake.

FAQ

Questions, answered straight.

Is Front Door HIPAA compliant, and will you sign a BAA?

Yes. PHI is processed in a secure, HIPAA-compliant environment with logged access and encryption in transit and at rest. We sign a BAA on every plan, including the free trial.

How does the AI handle a high-risk or crisis response?

Clinical Intake Automation flags high-risk responses in real time and alerts your clinical staff for rapid intervention. Escalation pathways are configured to your team's protocols.

What's the difference between the Virtual Assistant and Clinical Intake?

The Virtual Assistant works before booking, engaging, qualifying, and scheduling patients 24/7. Clinical Intake Automation works after booking, collecting assessments, history, and documents before the first visit. They run on one platform and hand off automatically.

Does it integrate with our EHR?

Yes. Front Door connects with EHRs and CRMs behavioral health teams run, including Kipu, BestNotes, KipuCRM, Lightning Step, Salesforce Health Cloud, and more. Exact capabilities depend on the system; we'll map it during onboarding.

Will the AI identify itself, and can a patient reach a human?

Yes. The assistant identifies itself and hands off to your staff whenever a human is needed or requested, passing along everything it has already gathered.

How fast can we go live?

Most practices are live within days. Onboarding includes a clinical scripting session, EHR connection, and a review period where your team approves conversations before the assistant goes fully live.
